Prologue — “Patch Notes: Destiny”

The office air conditioner hummed like a raid boss on easy mode. Outside, the city pretended it was normal. Inside, The MVP stared at a spreadsheet until the cells started to look like constellations.

That was when the desk lamp flickered—not broken, just… dramatic. A single holographic ticket printed itself in silence:

EVENT: Limited banner — Starlight
RATE UP: Mutual respect, soft laughs, legendary teamwork
WARNING: Feelings may crit for 2× damage

The MVP adjusted his glasses like a man about to speedrun his own heart.

Act I — The Summon Circle (Also Known As: The Break Room)

The break room microwave beeped with suspicious timing. Steam rose from a cup of something that was definitely “tea” in the same way a mecha is “just a vehicle.”

Starlight appeared like a post-credits scene: sudden, bright, and unfairly well-animated. She tilted her head. “You look like someone who carries the whole guild.”

The MVP tried to play it cool, which is difficult when destiny uses your lunch break as a cutscene. “I just… keep the quests on schedule.”

She smiled like she understood the grind—and like she had a secret skill tree for kindness. “Then let me be your party buff,” she said. “No tax. No interest. Just… here.”

Act II — Side Quest: Trust (Hard Mode, Worth It)

They saved the day in small ways: a corrected line item, a door held, a joke at the exact moment tension hit 99%. The MVP learned that Starlight hums OPs when she thinks nobody is listening.

She learned he keeps sticky notes that say things like “Remember to eat” and “Delegate like you mean it,” which is basically the romance version of tanking for the team.

One evening, the elevator lights strobed once, cinematic and suspiciously convenient. Starlight spoke softly. “You’re allowed to be tired and still be the hero, you know.”

The MVP exhaled a laugh he’d been holding like inventory space. “…Says the person who literally radiates main-character energy.”

“We can share the spotlight,” she said. “That’s what a good ship does.”

Act III — The Confession Patch (Stability Improvements)

Rooftop wind: cliché, yes, but clichés are just popular builds for a reason. The sky looked like somebody spilled neon ink and called it “atmosphere.”

The MVP stood at the railing like it was a podium. “I don’t know how you got into my storyline,” he admitted, “but you made the grind feel like co-op instead of solo.”

Starlight stepped closer—not a ambush, a choice. “Then accept this quest reward,” she teased, and took his hand like it was the rarest drop.

Fireworks went off somewhere in the distance, because of course they did. The universe respects a good rom-com timing window.

“No pressure,” she added, gentler. “Just… truth. You’re my favorite arc.”

The MVP nodded, eyes bright. “Same season pass,” he said. “Same canon.”

Epilogue — Monday, But Buffed

The office did not magically become a fantasy realm. The coffee was still suspicious. But now there was a second mug on the desk, and a shared calendar event labeled “Lunch (co-op).”

Starlight stole a glance over her monitor. The MVP stole one back. Both crit.

END — for now. Because the best ships get seasonal sequels. ✨
