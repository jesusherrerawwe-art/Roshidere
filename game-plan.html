Season Overview

Jesús Antonio "Chuy" Gutiérrez Fujioka arrives in Japan carrying nothing but a plastic bag of Pulparindos, a gold chain of the Virgen de Guadalupe, and a smile that could light up the darkest hallways of Seirei Private Academy. But behind that warmth lies a boy slowly losing his mother to grief, navigating a world that sees him as either a curiosity or a resource — and a girl who speaks in a language he can't understand, saying things she'd never dare say in Japanese.

Season 1 follows Chuy's journey from "tourist attraction" to a genuine presence at the academy — his friendships, his quiet heartbreak at home, and the moment when even the brightest smile finally cracks.

Character Compass

Protagonist

The Candy Boy. A grinder with a golden heart and a Virgen chain. He doesn't understand elitism — and that's his superpower. His warmth is both his shield and his blind spot.

Arc: Innocence → Belonging → Heartbreak → Silence

Deuteragonist

The Ice Princess. Refuses his candy but protects him from wolves. Mutters in Russian what she'll never say in Japanese. Watches everything — and sees too much.

Arc: Distance → Curiosity → Protection → Fury
👁️

Masachika Kuze

The Observer

Curious. Watchful. Drawn to Chuy's inexplicable ability to bypass Alya's walls. The third chair at the table — loyal, but always calculating.

Arc: Curiosity → Friendship → Witness

Maria "Masha" Kujou

The Bridge

The first to accept a Pulparindo. The first to welcome him. The warmth that tells Chuy this world isn't entirely cold.

Arc: Kindness → Anchor

"This charity case is trying to steal my brother's girlfriend." Protective of Kuze, protective of the academy's status quo. Her love becomes a weapon.

Chuy's mother. Drowning in grief and sake. Once a wonderful woman — still is, somewhere under the weight of Antonio's death. The hand that slaps is the same one that once held him.

Act I

ARRIVAL

"Nosotros los Nobles"

Chuy and Keiko arrive in Japan. A new world, a new school, and the first cracks in a mother-son bond that will define everything.

01

Sunday Night

✈️ Mexico → Japan 🛬 Narita Airport 🏙️ Tokyo Apartment

Opening image: The glow of a seatback screen. "Nosotros los Nobles" plays — a Mexican comedy about wealth and identity. Chuy watches, earbuds in, while Keiko sleeps beside him. This is his last moment as a Mexican kid. When the plane lands, he becomes something else: a foreigner.

Key Beats

  • The Lost Boy: While Keiko is in the restroom, Chuy spots a crying child in the airport corridor. The boy asks about his dark skin. Chuy jokes about it, breaking the tension. He takes the boy's hand and finds his parents near a terminal. The father offers yen. Chuy declines — "I only wanted to help."
  • First Sign of Decline: Chuy reunites with Keiko, excited to share what he did. She's on her phone. Barely listens. The camera (or the prose) lingers on her distracted face — this isn't just tiredness.
  • The Apartment: A luxurious city-center apartment. Keiko's personal driver (Takeshi) brought them. It's beautiful. It's empty.
  • The Shrine: Chuy hangs Antonio's photo in his room. Touches the gold Virgen de Guadalupe chain around his neck — his father's last gift. He'll never take it off.
  • Closing image: Chuy asleep. Keiko on her phone. Two people in one home, already worlds apart.
💛

Emotional Core: Chuy's first act in Japan is helping a stranger's child. Before we know his wealth, his grief, his heritage — we know his heart. That's the thesis statement.

02

Pulparindos

🏫 Seirei Private Academy

Opening image: Chuy at the school gates. Uniform on. Plastic bag in hand. Gold chain visible. A smile that doesn't know it's about to meet an institution built on hierarchy.

Key Beats

  • The Chain Confrontation: A teacher tells Chuy to remove the Virgen chain — accessories are forbidden. Chuy refuses, gently: "It's part of my identity. A gift from my father." He smiles so genuinely that the teacher makes an exception. First rule broken. First impression made.
  • Hallway Greetings: Chuy says "Good morning! Good luck!" to everyone he passes in the hallway. Students, janitors, teachers. Nobody knows how to respond. Confusion everywhere.
  • The Offering: In class, Chuy offers Pulparindos from his plastic bag. Most accept out of forced politeness. Alya refuses. Chuy, unfazed: "Not everyone can handle spicy treats." That smile.
  • First Friend: Masha welcomes him warmly. Accepts a Pulparindo gladly. The first genuine connection at Seirei.
  • Recess Reactions: Students who accepted the candy actually taste it — shock, surprise, some reluctant enjoyment. Flavor that doesn't compute with their palates. Doesn't mean friendship. But it means they'll remember him.
  • The Grinder: Chuy isn't the smartest in class. He's the one who tries hardest. "Échale ganas, mijo." Antonio's voice in his head.
🌶️

Emotional Core: Chuy doesn't understand "elitist school." He just sees people. That obliviousness is both his charm and his vulnerability — the walls don't exist for him because he can't see them.

03

The Limousine

🏫 Seirei Academy — Dismissal 🏙️ Tokyo Apartment

Opening image: The school bell rings. Chuy says goodbye to Masha — his only friend. He walks toward the exit, backpack on, Virgen chain catching the afternoon light.

Key Beats

  • The Reveal: Takeshi is waiting outside. In a limousine. Even by Seirei standards — where wealth is the baseline — this turns heads. The kid with the plastic bag and the spicy candy... rides in that?
  • Social Recalibration: Students immediately recalculate. The "weird foreign kid" becomes "the wealthy foreign kid." Some start scheming how to get close. The first predators circle.
  • Home Alone: Chuy arrives at the apartment. Alone. Takeshi drops him off. No Keiko. The apartment is beautiful. The apartment is empty. This becomes routine.
  • Day Two — Mazapán: Chuy returns with a different Mexican treat. This time, classmates accept almost immediately — some to impress him, a few genuinely curious. The social math has shifted overnight.
  • Alya's Sigh: From her seat, Alya watches the whole performance. She sighs. She knows these boys. She knows what's coming for Chuy. She still hasn't accepted a treat from him.
🚗

Emotional Core: The limousine is a double-edged sword. It gives Chuy visibility, but the wrong kind. The people who now want to be near him don't want him — they want what the limo represents. And Chuy, bless him, can't tell the difference.

Act II

FRIENDSHIP

"Échale Ganas, Mijo"

Chuy forms the bonds that will carry him — Alya, Kuze, Masha — while the bond that should hold him strongest continues to unravel at home.

04

The Bodyguard

🏫 Seirei Academy

Opening image: "Candy Boy." The nickname has stuck. Chuy hears it in the hallways. He doesn't mind — he thinks it's a compliment.

Key Beats

  • The Parasite: A student finally makes his move — flattery, compliments, a casual suggestion to ride in Chuy's limo after school. Chuy, oblivious, is about to agree.
  • Alya's Intervention: Alya appears. One look. One line. The student vanishes. It's not kindness — it's justice. She can't stand watching someone be used in front of her.
  • The Thank-You: Chuy thanks her sincerely. He doesn't fully understand what happened, but he knows she helped. Alya mutters something in Russian. The reader catches it. Chuy doesn't.
  • The Project: That same day, they're assigned a paired project. Chuy, without overthinking it: "We can work at my apartment." Alya hesitates. Agrees.
  • Yuki's First Glance: Yuki Suou notices Chuy for the first time. Intrigued. Not threatened — not yet. Just… watching.
🛡️

Emotional Core: Alya doesn't protect Chuy because she likes him. She protects him because watching someone be exploited in plain sight offends her sense of right and wrong. That distinction matters — it makes what comes later earned.

05

Esta Niña Habla Chistoso

🏙️ Chuy's Apartment

Opening image: Alya at the apartment door. She looks around — the luxury, the emptiness, Antonio's photo on the shelf. She says nothing. She notices everything.

Key Beats

  • Working Together: They sit across from each other. Research, notes, quiet focus. Professional distance. Then the cracks appear — small jokes, accidental eye contact, the electricity of two guarded people letting their guards slip, inch by inch.
  • The Language Game: Alya mutters in Russian — teasing, testing, saying things she'd never say in Japanese. Small confessions hidden in a foreign tongue. The Roshidere mechanic begins. Chuy hears the melody but not the meaning.
  • The Mirror: Chuy replies in Spanish: "Esta niña habla chistoso, me agrada." ("This girl talks funny. I like her.") Neither understands the other. Both just said something honest. The dramatic irony is doubled.
  • Dinner Alone: After Alya leaves, Chuy makes dinner. For one. He eats looking at Antonio's photo. The apartment is quiet.
🗣️

Emotional Core: Two people hiding behind languages the other can't understand, both saying kind things they'd never dare say in Japanese. This is the mirror mechanic — the moment this story becomes inseparable from the original Roshidere premise.

06

The Third Chair

🏫 Seirei Academy 🏙️ Chuy's Apartment

Opening image: Kuze approaches Chuy during lunch. Casual. Measured. But his eyes say: How did you get close to Alya without dying?

Key Beats

  • Kuze's Curiosity: Kuze befriends Chuy. He's drawn to the puzzle of him — a kid with zero social awareness who somehow bypasses Alya's walls like they don't exist. He asks about Chuy's mother. Chuy: "She's a wonderful woman." (He believes it.)
  • The Trio Forms: Next apartment session for the project. This time, Kuze joins — offering help, and keeping a close eye on both of them. Three chairs around the table.
  • Chuy Cooks: After finishing the project, Chuy insists on cooking. Mexican food. Real Mexican food. From-scratch Mexican food. He moves through the kitchen like Antonio taught him. Kuze and Alya exchange a look — this kid is serious.
  • The Dinner: They eat together. Laughter. Kuze's dry humor, Alya's reluctant enjoyment, Chuy's absolute joy at feeding people. For one evening, the apartment isn't empty.
🍳

Emotional Core: Cooking is Chuy's love language. He can't play political games. He can't articulate his feelings eloquently. But he can feed you. Food = love = Antonio's legacy. When Chuy cooks, his father is alive in the room.

07

Empty Plates

🏙️ Chuy's Apartment — Night

Opening image: The trio has left. Chuy washes dishes alone. He's saved a plate of food for Keiko, covered in foil, placed perfectly on the table. Waiting.

Key Beats

  • The Wait: Hours pass. Chuy does homework. Watches the door. Checks his phone — no messages from Keiko. The foil-covered plate sits untouched.
  • Keiko Arrives: Late. She walks past Chuy's open arms with barely a glance. She doesn't see the food. She doesn't look. A bottle of sake in her hand. She goes to bed.
  • The Delusion: Chuy tells himself she's tired. "She works so hard." He puts the untouched plate in the fridge. He'll offer it to her tomorrow. (She won't eat it tomorrow either.)
  • Parallel Cut — School: Next day at school, Chuy smiles like nothing happened. Masha, Kuze, Alya (when she's in the mood). The warmth machine keeps running. Nobody knows what happens when he goes home.
  • The Trend: Some students have started copying Chuy's style — bringing accessories to school (strictly forbidden). He's started a micro-revolution without trying. The administration is not pleased.
🍽️

Emotional Core: The plate of food Chuy saves for Keiko is the most heartbreaking prop in the story. It represents hope — she'll come home, she'll eat, she'll sit with me. Every untouched plate is a small death of that hope.

Act III

AMBITION

"Candy Boy"

Chuy's reputation crystallizes. He becomes more than a curiosity — he becomes a presence. And that's exactly what makes him dangerous in Yuki Suou's eyes.

08

Tourist Attraction

🏫 Seirei Academy

Opening image: Chuy walking through the hallways. Students nod. Some greet him. A few wave. The kid who couldn't get anyone's attention three weeks ago now gets it without trying.

Key Beats

  • The Reputation: "A dark-skinned boy with exotic sweets, a funny accent, and a smile that expresses naiveté and warmth." Basically, a tourist attraction. But there's something underneath the spectacle — something real. Some students are starting to sense it.
  • The Copycats: Students bring their own accessories, mimicking Chuy's chain-wearing rebellion. The school administration cracks down. Chuy didn't ask for this — he just wears what his father gave him.
  • The Exploiters (Ongoing): Others still try to use him. Favors, rides, connections. Chuy doesn't say no easily. Alya continues to run interference, each time with a deeper sigh and a sharper Russian mutter.
  • Masha's Steady Warmth: Through all of it, Masha remains constant. Not strategic, not calculating. Just kind. She and Chuy's friendship is the most uncomplicated thing in his life.
  • Study Sessions: Chuy grinds. Not brilliant, but relentless. "Échale ganas, mijo." His grades aren't spectacular, but they're climbing. Slowly. Steadily.
🎪

Emotional Core: Chuy's warmth is working. Not fast, but genuinely. The question is whether the real connections will outpace the fake ones — and whether Chuy can learn to tell the difference before it costs him.

09

The Suou Eye

🏫 Seirei Academy 👁️ Yuki's POV

Opening image: A shift in perspective. For the first time, we see through Yuki Suou's eyes. She's watching. She's always been watching.

Key Beats

  • Yuki's Assessment: She's noticed the hallway changes. The greetings. The accessories. The softening of Seirei's rigid atmosphere. And she's noticed the source — the "Candy Boy." Interesting. Concerning.
  • The Real Threat: But what really sets off her alarm isn't Chuy's influence on the school. It's his proximity to Alya. Yuki has plans for Alya — plans that involve her brother. Chuy doesn't fit those plans.
  • The Dark Thought: "This charity case is trying to steal Alya. He's trying to steal my brother's girlfriend. I can't allow it..."
  • Gathering Intelligence: Yuki begins quietly observing Chuy. His patterns, his weaknesses, his friendships. She's not attacking yet. She's mapping the terrain.
  • Irony: Chuy has absolutely zero romantic interest in Alya at this stage. He sees her as a friend — maybe even a guardian figure. Yuki's entire premise is wrong. But she doesn't know that. And it doesn't matter — because perception is power at Seirei.
🕷️

Emotional Core: This chapter recontextualizes the entire school dynamic. Everything we've seen as warm and organic — the friendships, the candy, the hallway changes — is now being viewed through a lens of threat assessment. Yuki's love for her brother is real. Her conclusions are wrong. That's what makes her dangerous.

10

The Election

🏫 Seirei Academy — Student Council

Opening image: A bulletin board. Student council election announcements. Chuy stops. Reads. Thinks. His eyes light up.

Key Beats

  • Chuy's Interest: He doesn't fully understand student politics. He just sees: a way to help people. A way to be part of something. He mentions it to Kuze and Alya. Kuze raises an eyebrow. Alya freezes — she knows what this will trigger.
  • Yuki's Alarm: Word reaches Yuki instantly. The Candy Boy wants to run for student council? Now it's not just about Alya — it's about institutional power. Chuy in the council threatens the status quo that Yuki's family sits atop.
  • The First Move: Yuki doesn't attack directly. She whispers. A comment here, a raised eyebrow there. "Does he even understand how things work here?" "Isn't it a bit soon?" "He's been here, what, a few weeks?" Seeds of doubt, planted with surgical precision.
  • Chuy Oblivious: He doesn't notice the undercurrent. He's too busy studying, bringing candy, greeting people. The political machinery grinding against him is invisible to a boy who doesn't believe in enemies.
  • Alya's Warning: Alya tries, in her indirect way, to warn him. A sharper-than-usual comment. A long look. She doesn't say "Yuki Suou is coming for you" — but the subtext is screaming it.
🗳️

Emotional Core: Chuy's desire to join the student council isn't about power. It's about belonging. He wants to contribute, to matter, to be part of the school that's slowly becoming his world. That sincerity is precisely what Yuki finds most threatening — you can't fight sincerity with logic.

11

Whispers

🏫 Seirei Academy 🏙️ Chuy's Apartment

Opening image: Chuy walks into school. A few students who used to greet him look away. Just slightly. Just enough.

Key Beats

  • Yuki's Campaign: The whispers are working. Nothing overt. Nothing traceable. Just a general sentiment shift. "He doesn't really belong here, does he?" "His grades aren't even that good." "It's all just because of his mother's money."
  • The Loyal Core: Masha, Kuze, and Alya remain unchanged. Masha pushes back against gossip when she hears it. Kuze is watching the political game unfold with sharp eyes. Alya says nothing — but her silence is its own weapon.
  • Cracks at Home: Keiko is spiraling. More sake. Later nights. Fewer words. Chuy still makes dinner for two. The second plate still goes untouched. He's running out of ways to delude himself.
  • A Private Moment: Late at night, Chuy holds the Virgen chain. Talks to it. Talks to Antonio. "Papá, estoy echándole ganas... pero todo se siente más pesado." ("Dad, I'm giving it my all... but everything feels heavier.")
  • The Pressure Builds: School isn't a sanctuary anymore. Home was never one. The only safe spaces are the trio, Masha's friendship, and the act of cooking. Everything else is starting to close in.
🌑

Emotional Core: The dual-antagonist structure reveals itself. Yuki is the external threat (school). Keiko is the internal threat (home). Chuy has nowhere safe. And he's still smiling.

Act IV

BREAKING POINT

"¿Por Qué No Moriste Tú?"

The smile cracks. The hand that once held him strikes. And in the silence that follows, a princess turns furious and an antagonist takes her throne.

12

The Slap

🏙️ Chuy's Apartment — Night

Opening image: Chuy at the kitchen table. Homework spread out. Two plates of dinner — one for him, one covered in foil. Waiting. Always waiting.

Key Beats

  • Keiko's Arrival: The door opens. Keiko stumbles in. The smell of sake precedes her. Her eyes are red. This isn't tiredness. This is a dam about to break.
  • The Confrontation: Chuy stands. "Mamá, te guardé—" He doesn't finish. Keiko is staring at him. Through him. Past him — to the ghost of the man she actually wants to see.
  • The Words: "¿Por qué no moriste tú en vez de Antonio?" — "Why didn't you die instead of Antonio?"
  • The Slap: Her hand connects with his face. The sound fills the apartment. Time stops.
  • The Aftermath: Keiko's face changes. Horror. She sees what she's done. She sees the red mark blooming on her son's cheek. She opens her mouth — but no apology comes out. She turns. She goes to her room. The door closes.
  • Chuy Alone: He stands in the kitchen. The foil plate is still on the table. His hand touches his cheek. His eyes are wide. The Virgen chain hangs against his chest. For the first time in the story, his face has no expression at all.
💔

Emotional Core: This is the scene that redefines everything. Every smile before it. Every smile after it. A mother told her son she wished he were dead. Those words are now inside Chuy forever. The chain still hangs. The photo still watches. But the boy who believed in both of them just got hit by the person he trusted most.

13

The Mark

🏫 Seirei Academy — The Days After

Opening image: The next morning. Chuy at the bathroom mirror. The slap mark is visible — red, fading to bruise. He tilts his head. Tries a smile. It doesn't come. He goes to school anyway.

Key Beats

  • The Silence Begins: No candy. For the first time since arriving at Seirei, Chuy walks through the hallways without a plastic bag, without greetings, without "Good morning!" He nods. That's it. The hallways notice.
  • The Group Feels It: Masha asks if he's okay. He says yes. His smile flickers but doesn't hold. Kuze studies him — he knows something shifted, but he doesn't push. Not yet.
  • Alya Sees: At some point during the day — maybe at lunch, maybe in the hallway — Alya catches it. The mark on his cheek. It's fading, but she sees it. Her eyes narrow. Her jaw tightens.
  • The Question: Alya corners him. Not gently. "Who did that?" Her voice is ice and fire simultaneously. Russian fury barely contained under Japanese composure. Chuy tries to laugh it off. "It's nothing—" She doesn't buy it. Not for a second.
  • No Answer: He won't tell her. He can't tell her. Because the answer is his mother. And admitting that out loud would make it real in a way he's not ready for.
  • Alya's Muttering: She turns away. Mutters in Russian. But this time it isn't teasing. It isn't playful. It's raw. Furious. Protective. Whoever did this to him will regret it.
🔥

Emotional Core: The moment Alya sees the mark is the moment the story shifts from "friendship" to something deeper. She doesn't know who hurt him. She doesn't know it was his mother. But she knows someone hurt the one person in this school who never hurt anyone. And that is unacceptable.

14

Season Finale — The Antagonist

🏫 Seirei Academy 🏙️ Chuy's Apartment

Opening image: Chuy's desk at school. No candy. No plastic bag. The Virgen chain is there — he'll never take it off — but the boy wearing it feels different. Smaller. Quieter.

Key Beats

  • The New Chuy: He goes through the motions. Classes. Notes. "Échale ganas, mijo." But the ganas feel mechanical now. The smile comes and goes like a light with a bad connection. He's not broken — but he's cracked.
  • The Group Holds: Masha brings him a treat. Something small, Japanese, sweet. A reversal. Kuze sits closer than usual. Doesn't talk more — just stays. Alya is a thundercloud. She hasn't stopped looking for the answer she demanded.
  • Alya and Chuy: A quiet moment. Maybe after school. Maybe in the empty classroom. She doesn't ask again about the mark. But she says something — in Russian, like always. Except this time, there's no teasing. No play. Just a sentence, spoken softly, that the reader understands and Chuy doesn't. Something like: "I won't let anyone hurt you again."
  • The Distance Shot: Yuki Suou, watching from across the hallway. She sees Alya close to Chuy. She sees the way Alya looks at him. She sees everything she feared — not because it's romantic (it isn't, not yet), but because it's real. And real threatens everything Yuki has built.
  • Yuki's Declaration: In her mind, or spoken to someone she trusts: "This has gone far enough." The chess pieces are set. The game plan is made. Yuki Suou steps into her role — fully, finally, without hesitation. The main antagonist has arrived.
  • Final Image: Chuy, at home. Alone. He doesn't make two plates anymore. He makes one. He sits at the table. Antonio's photo watches from the shelf. The Virgen chain catches the kitchen light. He eats in silence. But his hand touches the chain, and for a moment — just a moment — something in his eyes says: I'm still here.
⛓️

Emotional Core: Season 1 ends not with resolution but with a statement of survival. Chuy is hurt. Chuy is quieter. But Chuy is still wearing the chain. He's still showing up. He's still here. And three people — Alya, Kuze, and Masha — are standing closer to him than ever, even though they don't fully understand why he needs them to.

Meanwhile, Yuki has stopped watching and started moving. Season 2 is war.
